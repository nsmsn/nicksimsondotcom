Cedric Johnson on Public Goods and Inequality

Published 15 Jan 2021

Martin Luther King, Jr. Day is coming up. A national holiday commemorating the lives of people who made fighting inequality and ending poverty their life's work decades ago and still today. And today, amid a national recession, global pandemic, eviction crisis, etc. there is much work to do to continue King's legacy and fulfill his dream for the next generation.

I thought it would be appropriate to share and re-amplify an interview I found at the end of last year: Cedric Johnson on the Change Lab podcast from ArtCenter College of Design. Johnson is a professor of political science and African American studies at University of Illinois at Chicago and is joined in conversation with ArtCenter's president, Lorne Buchman. The podcast is about 50 minutes but covers a lot of topics: policing, education, inequality, and design and creativity.
